Key Features Explained

1. No New Taxes. No Tax Rate Hikes
The Finance Bill 2025 does not introduce any new taxes. Instead, it focuses on cleaning up outdated laws, simplifying the tax code, and making tax administration easier for both citizens and the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA). The goal is to create a fairer, more predictable tax environment.
No new taxes have been introduced in 2025
The focus is on making the tax system simpler and clearer
2. Tax-Free Per Diem Increased
Daily allowances (per diems) for employees working outside their duty station have been increased from Ksh 2,000 to Ksh 10,000 without attracting tax. This change reflects the real cost of meals, travel, and accommodation, especially with rising living expenses across the country.
Workers on official duty can now receive up to Ksh 10,000 per day tax-free
This benefits both public and private sector employees
Recognizes rising costs and supports field workers
3. Tea & Coffee Sector Support
VAT will no longer apply to packaging materials used in the tea and coffee sectors. This helps farmers, exporters, and processors by lowering their costs and boosting Kenya's competitiveness in global markets.
No VAT on tea and coffee packaging materials
Supports local farmers and exporters
Boosts Kenya's global tea and coffee market presence
4. Electric Transport Boost
Electric bicycles, buses, and lithium-ion batteries will now be exempt from VAT. This is part of Kenya's strategy to promote cleaner, more sustainable transport solutions and reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
No VAT on electric bikes, buses, or lithium batteries
Supports a cleaner, greener Kenya
Encourages shift to eco-friendly transport
5. Digital Economy Taxed Fairly
Non-resident individuals and businesses making money from digital platforms in Kenya (e.g., selling online or offering digital services) will now be taxed just like local businesses. This ensures fair competition and brings global digital companies into Kenya's tax net.
Foreign digital businesses must now pay tax on income earned in Kenya
Protects local businesses from unfair competition
Everyone earning from Kenya contributes fairly

Questions & Answers

How does the Bill help ordinary Kenyans?
The Bill provides multiple benefits including tax-free gratuity for retirees, increased tax-free per diems, automated tax reliefs, and protection from penalties due to system errors. It also removes VAT on essential items like electric transport and tea/coffee packaging.
What support does the Bill provide for businesses?
The Bill simplifies tax compliance, waives penalties for system errors, protects against double taxation, and reduces taxes on digital assets. It also provides clarity on tax assessments and extends time for VAT refund processing to ensure accuracy.
How does the Bill promote fairness in taxation?
The Bill ensures foreign digital businesses pay taxes like local ones, taxes luxury aviation, removes special rates for large housing developers, and requires KRA to explain assessment changes. It also prevents double penalties for tax withholding issues.
What environmental benefits does the Bill include?
The Bill exempts electric bicycles, buses, and lithium-ion batteries from VAT to promote cleaner transport. It also shifts tourism support from tax breaks to structural reforms for sustainable growth.
